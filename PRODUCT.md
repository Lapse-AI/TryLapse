# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Launch and QA operators, product engineers, and design reviewers who run pre-launch rehearsals and inspect issues before release. They work in time-boxed sessions and need quick confidence signals without digging through noisy output.

## Product Purpose
TryLapse helps teams rehearse critical user journeys, detect blockers and UX regressions, and decide launch readiness with evidence. Success means operators can identify what is broken, why it matters, and what to fix next in minutes.

## Brand Personality
Calm, subtle, professional. The interface should feel trustworthy and precise, with restrained visual styling that keeps attention on evidence and decisions.

## Anti-references
- Flashy growth-dashboard aesthetics with loud gradients and decorative visual effects.
- Over-carded enterprise templates that create depth noise and visual clutter.
- Hyper-minimal UIs that hide hierarchy and reduce scanability.

## Design Principles
- Evidence first: critical readiness signals and blockers stay obvious at a glance.
- Quiet hierarchy: use contrast and spacing deliberately, without visual aggression.
- Fast operator scanning: optimize layout for triage workflows and repeated use.
- Consistent semantics: status colors, labels, and patterns mean one thing everywhere.
- Trust through clarity: readable typography and straightforward copy over decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Use WCAG AA as the baseline for contrast and legibility. Keep interactions keyboard-friendly and preserve readable text sizes for dense operational screens. Avoid motion-heavy effects and ensure content meaning does not rely on color alone.
